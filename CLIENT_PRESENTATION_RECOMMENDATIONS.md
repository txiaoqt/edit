# Client Presentation Recommendations

## Overall Direction

Make the deck feel like a client product presentation instead of a capstone defense.

The main shift should be:

From: problem, legal context, and academic explanation  
To: client problem, proposed solution, app walkthrough, benefits, and implementation clarity

## Recommended Slide Flow

### 1. Title Slide

Keep the title slide, but shorten the title for easier client recall.

Suggested title:

**LYDO Connect**  
*A digital platform for youth programs, participation, and transparency*

### 2. Client Problem

Replace the long project context with three clear pain points:

- Youth information is scattered across different channels.
- Program participation is hard to monitor manually.
- Transparency records are difficult to organize and access.

### 3. Proposed Solution

Present LYDO Connect as the direct solution.

- Centralized youth information portal
- Online program and event registration
- Public transparency and financial monitoring
- Admin dashboard for managing records

### 4. Who Will Use the System

Add a simple role-based slide:

- **Citizens / Youth Users** - Browse programs, register for activities, submit concerns, and view transparency data.
- **LYDO / Authorized Staff** - Manage programs, events, records, and reports.
- **Administrators** - Manage users, roles, audit logs, and system data.

### 5. User Side Features

Keep the wording client-friendly and focused on what users can do:

- **Programs and Events** - Users can browse activities, view details, and register online.
- **Youth Organizations** - Users can discover and join LYDO-connected organizations and advocacy groups.
- **Profile and Participation Tracking** - Users can view and manage their joined programs, events, and organizations.
- **Citizen Desk** - Users can submit requests, complaints, suggestions, or information inquiries.
- **Transparency Reports** - Users can search, filter, view, and download public disclosure records.
- **Financial Disclosure** - Users can review barangay SK budget allocation, utilization, and remaining funds.
- **Barangay Map and Compliance Board** - Users can view barangay youth metrics, budget data, and compliance status.

### 6. Admin Side Features

Group admin features into fewer, clearer categories:

- **Dashboard and Monitoring** - Gives staff a quick overview of users, programs, events, organizations, and recent activity.
- **Programs, Events, and Registration Management** - Allows staff to manage activities and monitor participant registrations.
- **Organization and Barangay Data Management** - Helps staff maintain youth organization records and barangay-related data.
- **Transparency and Financial Management** - Supports disclosure documents, compliance records, and financial monitoring.
- **Citizen Desk Management** - Helps staff review, prioritize, and update citizen tickets and requests.
- **User, Role, and Audit Management** - Allows administrators to manage accounts, access levels, and activity logs.

### 7. App Walkthrough / Screenshots

Add actual screenshots of the system. This is one of the most important changes for a client presentation.

Recommended screenshots:

- Homepage
- Programs page
- Events or registration page
- Citizen Desk
- Transparency Reports
- Admin Dashboard
- Admin management modules

### 8. Benefits to the Client

Add a value-focused slide:

- Easier access to youth services
- Faster program and event monitoring
- More organized records
- Better transparency reporting
- Improved communication between LYDO and youth stakeholders

### 9. Scope and Limitations

Keep this slide, but soften the wording.

Suggested wording:

- The system supports LYDO operations but does not replace official government processes.
- It does not include payment processing, e-signatures, or full government database integration.
- Internet access is required for full system use.
- Features are focused on practical and budget-friendly digital support.

### 10. Project Clarifications

Shorten this slide and avoid making it sound like a warning.

Suggested wording:

- Developed as a capstone project prototype.
- Designed based on LYDO-related workflows and youth governance needs.
- Future deployment, ownership, and system turnover may be discussed through proper agreements.

### 11. Closing / Thank You

Use a stronger closing message:

**LYDO Connect helps make youth services more accessible, organized, and transparent.**

## Specific Fixes

- Change **Youth Desk** to **Citizen Desk** to match the actual site.
- Fix the footer typo: **Scienes** should be **Sciences**.
- Reduce long paragraphs into 3 to 5 clear bullets per slide.
- Add actual website screenshots to show the working system.
- Replace technical phrases with benefit-focused wording.
- Move academic terms like **ISO/IEC 25010** to a small note or remove them from client-facing slides.
- Do not overexplain the law; mention it only as brief context.
- Make the deck show how the app helps the client, not only how the project was developed.

## Most Important Slide to Add

### Why LYDO Connect Matters

- Centralizes youth programs, events, organizations, and transparency records.
- Allows youth users to access services and submit concerns online.
- Helps LYDO staff manage records, registrations, reports, and monitoring in one system.
- Supports better transparency, participation, and decision-making.

## Client-Readiness Assessment

The deck is aligned with the app's actual features, but it should be revised before presenting to clients.

Current readiness: **5.5 / 10**  
After content cleanup and screenshots: **7 / 10 or higher**

The strongest improvement would be to make the presentation more visual, concise, and benefit-focused.
